Vibrational control of thermally activated reactions in
the ground state by using time-resolved Raman spectroscopy

Observing the dynamics of proton transfer reactions in the ground state, needs a technique with a time resolution higher than what can be achieved by electronic means. A beneficial approach is pump-probe time-resolved spectroscopy to monitor the path that the proton takes on a very short timescale. It allows observing molecules during reactive processes, characterization of the associated dynamics, and searching for transition states and transient species. In these techniques, an ultrafast light pulse excites the sample and initiates the process of interest. A second ultrashort laser pulse probes the properties of the sample after a delay, which is the temporal separation between the two pulses. The measurement is repeated with systematically varied delay times to sample the complete evolution of an observable during the process under investigation.

Flow liquid jet system

Most ultrafast spectroscopy setup requires a flow system to present a new sample volume for each laser shot without windows in the optical path. The current wire-guided flow jet system in the spectroscopy setup used in our experiments consists of a nozzle, a wire guide loop, tubes, a liquid chamber, and a low-pressure micro gear pump.
